Max Hospital licence canceled--a strong step for the Aam Admi

AAP Government to Deliver 40 Public Services at Doorstep
test1 test2

Private hospitals have always looted the Aam Aurat, not just in Delhi, but everywhere in India. They are also making breaking the law--whether it is in providing free beds, or in complying with government orders for communicable diseases. In Delhi’s Shalimar Bagh, the Max Hospital was also negligent and declared an infant dead, when she was in fact alive, while demanding lakhs of rupees as fees for costly and illegal markups on consumables.

Hospital tragedies like this happen in other states--earlier in the year, a government hospital in Gorakhpur in Uttar Pradesh was in the news; recently a private hospital in Gurugram in Haryana. In UP, where the government has full powers, it filed criminal cases against its own staff

The Delhi government has limited powers; yet it did not hesitate to use what it had. Within days of this tragedy, the license of Max Hospital was canceled. That is because the AAP believes in sending a strong signal to all that wrongdoing will be punished--severely. This step was welcomed by all, including those outside the AAP family.

Unfortunately, the hospital has won a stay from the appellate authority, which comes under the Union government. The AAP believes that this happened because the rich, corporate hospital went to the BJP and ensured that it’s interests were met. Read more »

Banks and mobile companies provide these levels services to only their “privileged” customers. Delhi Government will provide all these and more services to all citizens of the State. A great example of #DelhiModel of Good Governance
